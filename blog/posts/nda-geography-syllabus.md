The NDA (National Defence Academy) exam includes Geography as part of the General Ability Test (GAT). Geography typically carries 15-20 questions worth 60-80 marks. Here's everything you need to know about the NDA Geography syllabus.

## NDA Geography Syllabus Overview

| Section | Topics | Weightage |
|---------|--------|-----------|
| Physical Geography | Earth, Atmosphere, Landforms, Climate | ~30% |
| Indian Geography | Physical features, Climate, Resources, Industries | ~40% |
| World Geography | Continents, Countries, Capitals, Important places | ~20% |
| Map Reading | Latitude, Longitude, Symbols, Scales | ~10% |

## 1. Physical Geography Topics

- **Earth:** Shape, size, rotation, revolution, seasons
- **Atmosphere:** Layers, composition, winds, pressure belts
- **Hydrosphere:** Oceans, currents, tides, waves
- **Landforms:** Mountains, plateaus, plains, volcanoes, earthquakes
- **Climate:** Types, factors affecting climate

## 2. Indian Geography Topics

- **Physical Features:** Himalayas, Plains, Peninsular Plateau, Islands
- **Rivers:** Major rivers, drainage systems, dams
- **Climate:** Monsoons, seasons, rainfall distribution
- **Resources:** Minerals, coal, petroleum, natural gas
- **Agriculture:** Major crops, green revolution, irrigation
- **Industries:** Iron & steel, textiles, IT hubs

## 3. World Geography Topics

- Continents and their features
- Major countries and capitals
- Important straits, gulfs, bays
- Mountain ranges and rivers of the world
- International boundaries and disputes

## Preparation Strategy

### Step 1: Build Strong Foundation
Start with NCERT Geography books from Class 6-12. Focus on Indian Geography first as it carries maximum weightage.

### Step 2: Practice Maps
Keep a blank map of India and World handy. Practice locating places, rivers, mountains daily.

### Step 3: Solve Previous Papers
Last 10 years' NDA papers show clear patterns. Some topics are repeated frequently.

### Step 4: Current Affairs
Stay updated on geographical events - natural disasters, new projects, boundary issues.

## Important Tips

- Focus on MCQ practice - NDA is all objective
- Learn locations, not just names
- Connect physical and human geography
- Remember key statistics (lengths, heights, areas)
